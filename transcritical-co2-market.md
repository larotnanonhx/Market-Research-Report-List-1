<p><p>Transcritical CO2 refers to a refrigeration system that utilizes carbon dioxide as a refrigerant in a transcritical cycle. Unlike conventional refrigeration systems that rely on hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), transcritical CO2 systems operate above the critical point of CO2. This technology is gaining popularity in commercial and industrial refrigeration due to its environmental benefits, including low global warming potential and compliance with stringent regulations targeting HFCs.</p><p>The Transcritical CO2 Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period. Additionally, advancements in technology, such as improved heat exchangers and control systems, are enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of transcritical CO2 systems. Recent trends indicate a growing adoption in various sectors, including supermarkets, food processing, and HVAC applications.
