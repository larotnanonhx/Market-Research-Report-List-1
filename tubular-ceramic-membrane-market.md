<p><p>The tubular ceramic membrane market encompasses two primary types: microfiltration and ultrafiltration. Microfiltration involves removing larger particles, such as suspended solids, bacteria, and some viruses, typically operating at pore sizes around 0.1 to 10 micrometers. In contrast, ultrafiltration targets smaller contaminants, including dissolved macromolecules and viruses, with pore sizes ranging from 1 nanometer to 0.1 micrometers. Both technologies leverage the durability and chemical resistance of ceramic membranes, making them ideal for various industrial applications, including water treatment and food processing.</p></p>
