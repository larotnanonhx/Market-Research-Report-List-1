<p><p>Microbial biosensors are categorized into various types based on their transduction methods. Electrochemical biosensors utilize electrical signals to detect microbial activity, making them highly sensitive and fast. Optical biosensors rely on light measurements to analyze microbial interactions, offering real-time monitoring capabilities. Piezoelectric biosensors detect changes in mass or frequency due to microbial growth, enabling label-free detection. Other market types may include thermal and magnetic sensors, broadening the application spectrum for detecting pathogens and microbial metabolites in various environments.</p></p>
