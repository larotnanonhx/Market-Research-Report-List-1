<p><p>The electric bus market primarily utilizes two types of lithium-ion batteries: Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) and Nickel Manganese Cobalt (NMC). LFP batteries are known for their safety, long cycle life, and thermal stability, making them suitable for urban transit applications. In contrast, NMC batteries offer higher energy density, allowing for longer ranges and faster charging, which is beneficial for intercity routes. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the efficiency and sustainability of electric public transportation systems.</p></p>
