<p><p>The solid-state cell market comprises three main types: polymer electrolytes, oxide electrolytes, and sulfide electrolytes. Polymer electrolyte cells utilize flexible polymers to enable ionic conduction, offering lightweight and safe options for various applications. Oxide electrolytes feature solid oxides that provide high conductivity and thermal stability, making them suitable for high-performance batteries. Sulfide electrolytes, known for their high ionic conductivity, offer enhanced energy density and stability, positioning them as promising candidates for next-generation energy storage solutions.</p></p>
